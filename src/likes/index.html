Physical products

I'm one of those people who's super particular about what they buy.

Clothing

  • Socks: Uniqlo Low-cut Socks at $5/pair. Yes, some socks are better than others. But should I pay $13 for a pair? Hell no! I've tried Bombas. I've tried Smartwool. I've tried Darn Tough. They're all fine. But they aren't even a little bit longer-lasting or more comfortable than these Uniqlo socks. Buy Uniqlo socks. Bombas, don't make me pay for your egregiously overprevalent marketing campaigns.
  • Boxer Briefs: Kirkland Signature Boxer Briefs at $4.25/pair. Likewise, I've tried a bunch of different brands of boxer briefs. Mack Weldon? MeUndies? These are all Power-MBA companies. They zero-in on easily-produced, high profit-margin items -- like, boxer-briefs, t-shirts, or socks -- make above-average products, and market them to hell. The problem with easily-produced items, of course, is that... well, they're easily produced. It's a crowded market, so they have to spend a lot on marketing to promote their brand. And who ends up paying for those huge marketing campaigns? That's right, you do. It's baked into the ticket price. Don't pay for marketing. Buy Costco.
  • Glasses: Warby Parker glasses at $95/pair. You can go cheaper. I actually own a few pairs from EyeBuyDirect that were priced between $35 and $75, and I like them almost as much as my Warby Parkers. But the quality is definitely a little bit lower. Warby Parkers are nicely designed, solid quality, and affordable. Sold sold and sold.
  • Jacket: Patagonia Nano-Puff Jacket at $225. I'm not exactly avant garde here. Plenty of people own this jacket. And frankly, I don't especially want anyone else to buy it. But it's so damned nice that I couldn't leave it out. This is the single most versatile piece of clothing I own. It's so compressible that you can pack it into your back pocket. It's surprisingly warm - comfortable until about 45 degrees F with a T-shirt underneath. And amazingly, despite how formless and lightweight it is, it doesn't look like crap when you wear it. It actually looks pretty good. I've worn this out to dinner, out to bars, to work functions. It'll work with button-ups and slacks, and it'll work with a shorts and a t-shirt.
